ABOUT US

WHAT IS KUMATHA?

An online platform that connects students, fabrication labs, and local suppliers to streamline prototype development from design to fabrication.

PROJECT ORIGIN

Developed to address students’ challenges in prototype fabrication by streamlining access to materials, quotations, and fabrication resources, thereby bridging academic projects with industry capabilities and fostering innovation.

MISSION

To be the leading platform that transforms student ideas into tangible prototypes by seamlessly connecting academia, fabrication labs, and local suppliers.

VISION

Committed to transforming student ideas into tangible prototypes by providing a seamless platform for access to materials, automated quotations, and coordinated fabrication services, fostering innovation, hands-on learning, and industry collaboration to enhance academic excellence and bridge the gap between education and real-world applications.

BOOKINGS

Step by step process

  • Upload Design – User uploads CAD model to Kumatha.
  • Select Material – Choose preferred material and specifications.
  • Request Processing – System sends design to partner suppliers for pricing.
  • Fabrication Scheduling – LIKHA FabLab schedules fabrication.
  • Quotation Finalization – Kumatha consolidates and finalizes quotations.
  • Cost & Time Confirmation – User receives cost and timeline estimate.
  • Prototype Fabrication – LIKHA FabLab fabricates the approved design.
  • Inspection & Quality Check – Prototype undergoes testing and verification.
  • Delivery – Completed prototype delivered to user or team.
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
What materials are available for prototyping?
+

We offer a wide range of materials including plastics (PLA, ABS, PETG), metals (aluminum, steel), and composites. Availability depends on your project requirements and supplier partnerships.

How long does the prototyping process take?
+

Timelines vary based on complexity, material, and fabrication method. Simple prototypes can take 1-3 days, while more complex ones may require 1-2 weeks. We'll provide a detailed estimate during the quotation process.

What file formats do you accept for designs?
+

We accept CAD files in formats such as STL, OBJ, STEP, and IGES. Ensure your files are high-resolution and include all necessary details for accurate fabrication.

Is there a minimum order quantity?
+

For prototyping, we typically handle single units or small batches. Contact us for custom orders or larger quantities.

How do I get a quote?
+

Upload your design, select materials, and submit your request through our platform. We'll process it and provide a consolidated quotation from our partner suppliers.
